The list of National Basketball Association career scoring leaders
records the players who have scored the most total points during their
NBA careers and reflects the evolution of scoring across different eras
of professional basketball. Throughout league history several legendary
players have accumulated extremely high point totals through long
careers consistent scoring and offensive dominance. LeBron James
currently stands among the most significant figures in this record list
after surpassing Kareem Abdul-Jabbar to become the all time leading
scorer in NBA history, an achievement widely discussed in modern sports
media coverage during the 2023 and 2024 seasons. Kareem Abdul-Jabbar
held the scoring record for nearly four decades after retiring with more
than thirty eight thousand points, largely accumulated through his
signature skyhook shot and exceptional longevity. Karl Malone ranks near
the top of the historical list as well, having built his scoring total
through years of offensive consistency with the Utah Jazz where he
frequently converted pick and roll opportunities alongside John
Stockton. Kobe Bryant also appears prominently among the career scoring
leaders, remembered for his scoring versatility competitive mentality
and remarkable twenty year career with the Los Angeles Lakers. Michael
Jordan remains one of the most influential scorers in league history
even though his career total is lower than some players who benefited
from longer careers; his scoring efficiency and multiple scoring titles
helped define the modern offensive superstar model. Dirk Nowitzki
revolutionized scoring among big men with his perimeter shooting and
fadeaway jump shot, allowing him to accumulate more than thirty thousand
points while spending his entire career with the Dallas Mavericks. Kevin
Durant is another modern era scorer who continues to climb the
historical scoring list thanks to his combination of height shooting
range and offensive efficiency. Other notable names frequently included
in discussions of career scoring leaders include Shaquille O Neal Wilt
Chamberlain Moses Malone Carmelo Anthony and Hakeem Olajuwon each of
whom dominated scoring during different periods of NBA history. Stephen
Curry although known primarily for transforming the three point shot
into a central component of modern offense has also accumulated a
rapidly growing career scoring total as his career progresses through
the 2020s. Changes in league rules playing style and offensive spacing
have influenced scoring totals over time, with the modern pace and three
point era enabling higher scoring outputs compared with some earlier
defensive periods. Despite these shifts the career scoring leaderboard
still reflects extraordinary longevity durability and scoring skill
because maintaining high point totals over many seasons requires both
physical resilience and sustained offensive impact. In contemporary
basketball analysis career scoring leaders are often evaluated not only
by total points but also by scoring averages efficiency metrics and
playoff contributions which together provide a more complete picture of
a player’s offensive legacy. The continued progression of active players
such as LeBron James Kevin Durant and other modern stars means that the
ranking of career scoring leaders continues to evolve as each season
adds new totals to the historical record of the National Basketball
