Redwood National and State Parks located along the northern California
coast in the United States form a protected network of forests rivers
and coastline that preserve some of the tallest trees on Earth including
the coast redwood Sequoia sempervirens which can grow higher than one
hundred meters. The park complex includes Redwood National Park together
with Prairie Creek Redwoods State Park Del Norte Coast Redwoods State
Park and Jedediah Smith Redwoods State Park which are cooperatively
managed by the National Park Service and California State Parks. In
recent years conservation programs have focused on restoring damaged
ecosystems improving salmon habitat along river systems such as the
Smith River and Klamath River and protecting old growth redwood forests
from climate change impacts including wildfire risk drought and shifting
rainfall patterns. Visitors commonly explore scenic areas such as Fern
Canyon Tall Trees Grove and the Redwood Creek watershed while hiking
cycling wildlife viewing and learning about the cultural history of
Indigenous communities including the Yurok Tolowa and Karuk peoples who
have lived in the region for thousands of years. Redwood National and State Parks on the northern California coast
