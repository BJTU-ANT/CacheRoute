The 2017 BBC Sports Personality of the Year Award was won by British
Formula One driver Lewis Hamilton, who secured the honor after claiming
his fourth Formula One World Championship during the 2017 season. The
annual BBC ceremony celebrates major sporting achievements by athletes
connected with the United Kingdom. In the 2017 vote Hamilton finished
ahead of Olympic and world champion distance runner Mo Farah and
para‑athlete Jonnie Peacock. The event, broadcast live by the BBC, also
highlighted team achievements, coaching contributions, and lifetime
sporting impact. In later commentary and recent coverage, Hamilton’s
victory is often remembered as part of his dominant era in Formula One,
which continued into the early 2020s and reinforced his reputation as
one of the most successful drivers in the history of the sport.
